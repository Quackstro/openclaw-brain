**Market Size (Tampa + Atlanta):**

- Per-job pricing: $150–$500 per property (photos + video + edited output)
- Premium package with 3D mapping: $500–$1,500
- Monthly retainer with real estate brokerages: $1,000–$5,000/month
- **Realistic Year 1:** $50K–$150K (100–500 jobs)

**Technical Feasibility:** ★★★★★ EASY
- Standard drone photography with existing camera payloads
- AI can auto-generate shot lists based on property boundaries
- Automated flight paths around properties
- Dashboard integration for delivery to clients

**Time to Market:** Immediate (0–3 months)
- Requires: Part 107 certification, camera gimbal, basic photo/video editing pipeline
- AI enhancement: auto-editing, virtual staging recommendations

**Synergy with Core:** MEDIUM — Uses same drone hardware but different AI models. Can cross-sell security monitoring to real estate clients with vacant properties.

**Competition Level:** HIGH — Crowded market with many small operators and established players. **Differentiation:** Automated/AI-driven reduces cost; bundling with security monitoring is unique.

**Competition Level:** MEDIUM — Established surveying firms but slow to adopt drones. Price advantage is significant (drone surveys cost 50–75% less than traditional).

## 8. Regulatory Considerations

### FAA Regulations (Drones)

| Requirement | Status | Impact | Timeline |
|------------|--------|--------|----------|
| Part 107 Certification | Required for all commercial operations | Must have certified remote pilot | Immediate (study + test takes ~2 weeks) |
| Part 107 Night Operations | Allowed with anti-collision lighting | Most security patrols are at night | Immediate (lighting required) |
| BVLOS Waiver | Required for utility corridor, large-area operations | Limits autonomous range without waiver | 3–6 months for waiver approval |
| Operations Over People | Category 1–4 based on drone weight/design | Needed for event security, public areas | Varies by category; Category 1 (<0.55 lbs) immediate |
| Remote ID | Required as of March 2024 | All drones must broadcast ID | Immediate (compliance required) |

### State and Local Regulations

| Jurisdiction | Key Regulation | Impact |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Florida | §934.50 — Freedom from Unwarranted Surveillance Act | Restricts drone surveillance by private entities; exceptions for property inspection with consent |
| Florida | No state-level drone pre-emption | Individual municipalities may have drone ordinances |
| Georgia | O.C.G.A. §6-1-4 — Drone operations | State pre-empts local drone laws; relatively drone-friendly |
| Tampa | MacDill AFB airspace restrictions | Large portions of Tampa Bay require LAANC authorization |
| Atlanta | Hartsfield-Jackson airspace | Significant airspace restrictions in south Atlanta |

### Insurance and Liability

- Commercial drone insurance: $500–$1,500/year per drone
- General liability for robot operations: $2,000–$5,000/year
